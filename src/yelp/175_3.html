This is a review for the Labor and Delivery unit, New York's best kept secret. It's newly renovated, run by midwives, gives every new mother her OWN huge private room (that's a big deal for Nyc in case you didn't know). They really push you to breastfeed, which might feel like bullying at first because breastfeeding is just so hard but they help you, encourage you and make sure you are taken care of. You are in such good hands at this hospital. I would tell every expecting mother to have her baby here. More women need to know about this gem of a hospital. Please avoid this institution at all costs! I regret my decision to use this hospital when I had a bone break in my foot - even though it was literally across the street from my home. Expect to not be given more than one option for treatment ever... you have to do your own research and fight with them if you diverge from their prescribed path. From the ER they assigned me to a "resident" - a doctor in training. My month-later follow-up took 4 1/2 hours - with no more than a half hour of actual treatment time. After two months, when the x-ray showed that the fracture had finally healed, the resident doctor told me cheerfully, "You're good! Now you can wear a regular shoe!" - and no further instructions other than the general "avoid strenuous activity". But I couldn't walk! They sent me out of there with no follow-up plan, no physical therapy, nothing. So back at home I had to start over again, trying to find a referral as quickly as possible through the primary care doctor they had assigned me to prior to the injury. Four separate calls trying to navigate through the convoluted phonemail system until I finally reach a nurse who tells me to come to the hospital early in the morning to speak to my doctor's nurse. So the next day I come extra-early, wait until the staff actually arrive, and I'm the second person in line to explain my purpose and wait to be called. I take out my book and prepare to wait. FIVE HOURS later I start to wonder what's going on, and take my place in line to speak to the receptionist again. She takes my card and says she'll try to help me but has to help all the other people waiting on line. An hour later she finally calls me to speak to a nurse, who says my doctor isn't even here today, and gives me an appointment for a week later. This is all just to get the referral for physical therapy that I should have been given in the first place. So that's six hours in a hospital waiting room to just speak to a nurse. They never would have called me at all had I not spoken to the receptionist a second time. How is this an acceptable way to treat a patient?

horrible, dirty, unclean, unprofessional "hospital". i've yet to see a mcdonalds or a gas station bathroom that's dirtier than this place, please avoid at all costs, i felt like i was catching a disease just being in there. it's like they've never heard about keeping the place sterile. long waits/overcharging for visits/etc. i can assume to encounter at the er, but a dirty (poop smell all over) waiting room is just not something that should be allowed in a hospital
