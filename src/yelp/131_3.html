    It started with a strange pain I got one morning while 6 weeks pregnant that extended throughout the whole day while I went to work. After I felt like I'm losing consciousness midday at work, I called my husband and told him I need to go to ER. Unfortunately Bellevue was the closest and not knowing much about it, we decided to go there. This is where the saga of trying to have the staff take my pain and condition seriously began.

    The receptionist in the waiting room all but scoffed at us when we said I need to be seen immediately, saying "I see people here who are much worse off than you are." Of course I didn't know that I was actively bleeding inside already, but I knew that my pain was abnormal, i couldn't stand straight and it was getting worse. Thankfully my husband left reception, found some manager and imparted on him that he is dealing with a lunatic husband of a pregnant lady who is being denied immediate care and shit is going to hit the fan pretty soon. The receptionist got a phone call a minute later and I was immediately admitted.

    The ER floor was a whole other ballpark. There was a belligerent drunk guy literally handcuffed to the stretcher screaming his head off, a disheveled woman obviously still high on something wondering around with an IV bumping into things. I would have thought it was funny if I wasn't in pain. They parked me on a stretcher in the hallway, and the next doctor/nurse I saw tried really hard to convince me that perhaps the pain was "gas". SERIOUSLY?!! I know what gas feels like, that wasn't it. Meanwhile I described symptoms of ectopic pregnancy to a T. To make a long story short, while in ER I was seen by several ER doctors and they even did a couple of Â ultrasounds. We were admitted at 3pm, by 7pm noone still has any clue whats going on, while my pain is getting worse and worse and I'm having trouble breathing. Meanwhile my husband has to evade the security guard who tries to get him to leave the whole time, while trying to get me attention of medical staff. I am still not being taken seriously after I lose consciousness while trying to use the bathroom a couple of times.

    FINALLY at 10PM!!!! I get to see an ACTUAL obgyn. She does an ultrasound and is HORRIFIED that I haven't been operated on yet. She says my stomach is full of blood. Great. They rush me to surgery, and at this point everything is finally handled in a professional manner. Turns out I had a ruptured abdominal pregnancy, which is even more rare, dangerous and life -threatening than ectopic pregnancy. Thanks Bellevue for my brush with death.

    On a positive note, the doctors who operated on me and the staff in the recovery floor were very nice, thorough and professional. AVOID ER AT BELLEVUE at all costs!!
