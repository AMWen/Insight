The care is fine, the low rating is for the billing process. We took my son to the ER for a strange rash when his doctor's office wasn't open. That went fine. We had a problem with my insurance. Because of an enrollment error by my insurer my son came up listed as uninsured. It took a few weeks to be resolved. We kept receiving bills for the visit so I informed Beth Israel in writing that it was an insurance error. A statement came back that the bill was paid when the insurance went through, so I thought all was fine until I started receiving calls from a very rude woman from a COLLECTION AGENCY. So I looked up Beth Israel on the internet and found an e-mail address for the billing department. I explained the situation and asked them to call off their dogs. After about four back and forth emails the woman finally told me that she does not handle billing for Beth Israel Needham, only the one in Boston. Despite the fact that I said the visit was at Beth Israel Needham in the very first e-mail. I then called Beth Israel Needham and they said their bill was paid. I said but I keep getting calls from the collection agency. She said that the bill might be from the doctor, not the ER. What? After calls to my insurer, etc. I found out that the hospital generates one bill while the doctor that saw my son generated a separate bill. Even though I clarified the billing problem with the hospital they do not share information with THEIR OWN DOCTOR. And the doctor, instead of resubmitting it or attempting to resolve it with, um, the information in their own hospitals data system, sent my name to a collection agency. I live in Needham, for Pete's sake. In Massachusetts. Where we have mandated universal healthcare. What is more likely? I don't have insurance or a billing error? Before all this I had a credit score in the 900's. Now I have to monitor my credit report to make sure that this isn't going to show up and destroy my credit. The care is fine but there is another Harvard Med School teaching hospital just down the road. And they've never sent me to a collection agency. Next time I'm going to Newton Wellesley.

Worst emergency room I've ever been to. I took my friend there because of severe hip pain. They didn't take one look at her before wanting to discharge her, and refused to take an x ray of her hip. When we finally got them to take an xray, they did it in 10 minutes, and sent her off saying she was fine. The next day my friend went to another doctor and said the xray showed a clear break. My friend clearly needed surgery.

Not sure if it was off hours or what, but I was admitted to the emergency room and the experience was very pleasant (as pleasant as a visit to the ER can be). I was seen within 15 minutes, they helped with insurance as I was in between carriers at the moment, and they were thorough and clear the entire time I was there. Everyone I came in contact with was professional and friendly.

I have been spending a lot of time in different hospitals lately with two sick family members. THIS Is the WORST! They solved the issue we came in for but then convinced us that more testing should be done and after over three hours still waiting because all test results are sent to Boston to read! Bull crap! If the MD REALY thought there was a problem the test would be read sooner !!! Just looking to pad the bill at the expense of people that have already been through hell. Stay away . You can drive to Boston and be treated and get home sooner.

I was admitted around nine in the morning after a night of violent stomach problems (no need to go into detail). The hospital was empty and they took me right away. The nurse, Nancy, was thorough with my intake, quick and very attentive. The doctor decided I needed two IVs to replace fluids. My entire visit lasted two and a half hours and I was only released when they were sure I had the strength to make it home. The best part was the warm blankets they laid on top of me to help me fall back asleep and the constant cups of ice chips. For two seconds I felt like I was at a spa. [Those passed quickly.] If you don't want to deal with a huge hospital but still want the same caliber of care, I recommend Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center.

My 90 year old mother fell today and was taken to the Beth Israel in Needham. Wow, I've never experienced ruder nurses in my life. The medical care seemed to be adequate but the gruff attitude was incredible. My mom has Alzheimer's and when I tried to tell the nurse to give ME the instructions on aftercare etc, she barked that she would explain it to her. Of course my mother got confused and the nurse was horrible. As well, sitting at the nurses stations were no less than 6 nurses and when I approached them with a question, they barely acknowledged me. Within 10 mins, a doctor walked over and shouted "could someone PLEASE help me in here. She was clearly frustrated with the nurses and the clearly were not interested in assisting myself, or the doctor. As I said, I don't really know about the medical care to any degree but I do know, the nursing staff, in the emergency room, Jan 12th were like "couch potatoes". It was pathetic. Unless your truly in an emergency situation and can go nowhere else, I'd avoid this hospital, for a hospital that can display the name "Beth Israel", they're and embarrassment.
