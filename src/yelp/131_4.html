Wellll, if any establishment should lay your uncertainty to rest it should be a hospital. I went to visit my patient on a weekday evening after work and with little trouble, made it to the ICU. Bellevue had misspelled my patient's name, a rather egregious error for a hospital, but the front desk was aware of it and informed me and other visitors of the mistake. I got to the room and within a few minutes, met all my patient's providers (nurses/doctors) and other visiting members. With the family's permission, I was apprised of my patient's condition and all my questions were answered. I have worked at several hospitals and never once had other providers been as communicative as the staff here was (social workers also generally get treated as second-class citizens). Though my patient was critically ill, it appeared as if the providers were doing everything they could to ensure my patient was as comfortable as possible. I left with their contact info. and left mine with them... not to mention a HUGE sense of relief. All in all, everyone I witnessed working on the floor did their best with my patient and others. I would highly recommend Bellevue to friends, family and patients! Also, Dr. Ian Fagan and Dr. Cassia Hill did a phenomenal job - healthgrades does not do their knowledge of medicine, ability to communicate with laymen or bedside manner justice. If I could give Bellevue an emoticon, thumbs up!
