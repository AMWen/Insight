    Beware !   This hospital and some of the doctor's in this hospital in my opinion are totally incompetent. I was referred to Norwood hospital ER by a local urgent visit clinic to receive IV antibiotics for an infection in my foot. Entered the ER around 1:00 and was admitted around 5:00.  Was told the infectious disease "specialist" would be attending to me that evening and that they would do an MRI of the foot.  More than 24 hours later I was finally visited for less than 5 minutes by this specialist, who looked at the foot and said it is doing fine you need to keep it raised.  Barely touched the foot, and when he did his hand was shaking so much that he couldn't even take a sample of a small blister that had formed on top of the foot. I stayed on IV antibiotics for another 3 full days as the foot and then the leg became more inflamed, swollen and blistering.  The specialist came in for 3-5 minutes per night and each time said it was getting better.  No other testing or MRI was done until I threatened the attending house doctor that I was going to discharge myself the next morning if there wasn't testing done to determine what type of infection I had and why it was getting worse not better. He, not the specialist proceeded to order an MRI and other blood cultures.  The infectious disease specialist came in for his 5 minute exam that evening and I asked him "what the purpose of the MRI was" and he gave me this arrogant answer "I didn't order the test" and walked out, then a few minutes later he came back in (evidently had seen the results) and said he was ordering a surgical consult for the next morning, no explanation, no discussion.  The next morning a very good surgeon did come in to see me and told me that he needed to take care of my foot right away and asked why he hadn't been called in days ago.  That morning he opened up my foot and removed a very large abscess.  I continued on IV antibiotics for another 4 days (total of 8 days in the hospital for something that should have been diagnosed and treated in 2 days) and spent another 10 days at home on strong antibiotics and in significant pain.  More than a month later I still have nerve damage pain and I'm just starting to walk somewhat normally now.  Then to top it off I received a letter the day after I got discharged that the "Specialist" was out of my network and I would be paying his bill out of my pocket.  
    I learned a few things:  
    You need to ask if any doctors that are seeing you are out of your plan. Even though they take all your insurance information they don't have to tell you some of those details.
    Don't assume that your doctor's have your best interest in mind or that they are competent even though they are called "specialist".  
    Find a better hospital, nurses were good, janitorial staff were fine, etc.  
    However, the doctors who were charged with protecting my health and well being were either not engaged or totally incompetent with the exception of the surgeon who was called in much too late !
