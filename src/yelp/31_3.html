Specialties

Emerson Hospital is a regional medical center that provides a wide range of advanced medical services to more than 300,000 people in 25 towns. With a Main Campus located in Concord, Massachusetts, we're a 179-bed hospital with more than 300 primary care doctors and specialists.

History

Established in 1911.

In 1910, Charles Emerson, nephew of Ralph Waldo Emerson, donated 80 acres of land and $20,000 to establish a hospital on the banks of the Sudbury River in Concord. The gift established a 14-room "cottage" that was dedicated as the Concord Deaconess Hospital on November 12, 1911. In 1924, the hospital was renamed after Mr. Emerson.
