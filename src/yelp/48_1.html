Fantastic ER! I had the pleasure (?) of popping through on Saturday evening after a quick 'trip' down the stairs. 1) There's a valet (perfect for the ER when I want OUT of the car as soon as possible); garage parking was also available, though I'm not sure if this building connected to the ED - cost for ~3hr = $5 2) The lobby/waiting area is spacious, recently renovated, and clean; vending machines include soda, protien shakes, water, gatorade, candy, chips, ice cream, hot pockets, and White Castle hot dogs (...what?) 3) Security ushers you through a metal detector and bag check before you check-in (great! except I didn't really enjoy going *back out* to the waiting area, then *back in* through the detector again when it was my turn to be seen in a room). These folks got me a wheelchair stat (which was appreciated) and a receipt for a pocket knife that I carry everywhere (woops). 4) Friendly faces when you're checking in (Megan was SO nice) 5) Short wait - though this can vary depending on the reason and time of your visit (you never know with an ED) - I checked in, was triaged, and was taken to a room within *maybe* an hour (not bad for Saturday 7pm!) 6) Fast, efficient, and kind care - shout out to Dr.James Fidrocki! He was SO kind and calm that I was completely at ease. Within about an hour I had seen the good doctor, been transported to X-ray (the techs weren't especially friendly, but whatever), transported back to my comfy ED bed, and then received my diagnosis (broken 5th metatarsal - ouch). Received the diangosis and treatment plan from Fidrocki, received my paperwork, and checked out within THREE hours! Not bad! Other than the reason why I was visiting (ouch), I really enjoyed the UMass Memorial ED. It's clean and updated and all of the care I've received here (well....other than the xray techs) was fantastic and filled me with confidence - that's something you want when you're in pain and urgently need help. Yes, it can be a wait sometimes (particularly in the height of flu season) - but you want the BEST care possible.....and for that, my friends, sometimes you need to recognise your surroundings and hold out for your turn.
