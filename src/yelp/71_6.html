The first few times we walked right in and didn't have to sign in once we told them what we were there for, but then the last two times they made us register and sign in, which is a complete waste of time in my opinion, ESPECIALLY the last time when I was in labor. They need to be more consistent with their procedures; either you have to sign in ALL times, or you don't have to sign in EVER. Not just whenever the security guard decides to enforce the rules.

    But I digress.

    They have a good number of recovery rooms after labor, but they are all two bed rooms. There are only THREE private rooms and they are a first come, first serve basis. I wish they enforced a pay for the private room kind of rule, because I would've gladly paid extra to not have to share my room with someone, but I suppose it's to be fair to those that can't afford a private room. I was also told when I pre-registered at the hospital that there are XX number of two bed rooms and that most of the times they will put one patient in one room before they start doubling up, which was DEFINITELY NOT THE CASE FOR ME.

    Now, before you think that I'm such a pansy and complainer, I had a last minute c-section plus my son had to be placed in NICU for a few days. It would've helped the recovery process if I had my own space and my husband was able to stay over and help me take care of the baby. Once my son was out of NICU, I was fully expected to climb out of bed and feed/change/attend to him, which I would've gladly done had I not just gone through a major surgery.

    There are some nurses that are more competent than others (thank you Miss Ha and Anzhella) while others just don't really give a damn. Some will look at you as if you're stupid and senile when you ask questions; hello, I don't do this everyday, I'm bound to ask what you may deem as "stupid" questions okay. I was very uncomfortable with some of the nurses and couldn't wait for their shifts to be up, which is sad.

    A word of advice for other moms-to-bes: Make sure you take full advantage of the nurses, lactation consultants, residents... they are there to help you. Once you go home, you are really on your own. Also, make sure you stock up on hospital freebies -- we were able to get formula, diapers, pads, Medela parts to take home with us.
