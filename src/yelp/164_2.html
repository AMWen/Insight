The experience started at the garage, since we weren't in full rush mode and didn't have to use valet service though it is available and from what I can make out costs about the same ($6 per day)...don't quote me on that. I don't understand why a hospital that charges thousands thinks it's necessary to also add a fee to their garage. It's like charging the new dad's for their horrific coffee (which they do, as well)... Back to our story. Once in the hospital lobby, you must locate the proper color elevator. Instead, we just asked front desk who pleasantly directed us with a smile and "congratulations". We made our way up to two of the nicest triage nurses (or whoever checks you into triage) and let into a bleak exam room to determine if I needed to stay or could be sent home. After a couple hours, and most of that time spent walking the halls of the much less bleak birthing center, we were led to a private delivery room. It was spacious and well appointed. A convertible chair in the corner turned into a twin bed for my husband. It looked like it came from the Cold War. Post delivery accommodations were identical, just missing equipment used for delivery. Every nurse I encountered was comforting and confident. I appreciated having the same nurse that helped in delivery afterwards the next day in recovery. The lactation consultants were helpful without being pushy, though I was already on board their team. With my first son (born out of state), the consultants were.....rude, is probably the best way to put it. Here, everyone seemed genuinely happy to help you with whatever choices you made. One of those choices for us was getting an early discharge. Everyone was happy to help move us along so we were able to come home about 30 hours after delivery. It was an "ideal" uncomplicated delivery and my newborn boy is the picture of health, so it was just a matter of standard tests and paperwork to get through. Even though, or perhaps because, the center was full, we were ready to head home even earlier than originally expected. It would be a 5 star experience if it weren't for the food. Yeah, I know it's called room service just because it comes to your room, but don't ever think of those trays as bringing anything close to actual resort food. Again, with what your bill ends up being, you'd think a bit more could be done about the little things. The food honestly played a role in our decision to come home a day early. It was all bad. Except for the cinnamon roll, which was supposed to be a croissant, but the roll was ok. Like a decent packaged gas station pastry.     Hospitals are odd. Obviously you want doctors and nurses capable of treating whatever ailments you may have, but considering the cost of your stay, we often think all aspects of our visit should be top notch. It's definitely the priciest room I've rented. Can you imagine what an amazing view it would have if it were a resort in the South Pacific?? Alas, it is not. It is a hospital in cold New York. And the views from my rooms were of other buildings. I think I saw a bird once.

    My visit was a happy one as I checked into the birthing center to have my second baby. The experience started at the garage, since we weren't in full rush mode and didn't have to use valet service though it is available and from what I can make out costs about the same ($6 per day)...don't quote me on that. I don't understand why a hospital that charges thousands thinks it's necessary to also add a fee to their garage. It's like charging the new dad's for their horrific coffee (which they do, as well)...

    Back to our story.

    Once in the hospital lobby, you must locate the proper color elevator. Instead, we just asked front desk who pleasantly directed us with a smile and "congratulations". We made our way up to two of the nicest triage nurses (or whoever checks you into triage) and let into a bleak exam room to determine if I needed to stay or could be sent home. After a couple hours, and most of that time spent walking the halls of the much less bleak birthing center, we were led to a private delivery room. It was spacious and well appointed. A convertible chair in the corner turned into a twin bed for my husband. It looked like it came from the Cold War. Post delivery accommodations were identical, just missing equipment used for delivery.

    Every nurse I encountered was comforting and confident. I appreciated having the same nurse that helped in delivery afterwards the next day in recovery. The lactation consultants were helpful without being pushy, though I was already on board their team. With my first son (born out of state), the consultants were.....rude, is probably the best way to put it. Here, everyone seemed genuinely happy to help you with whatever choices you made.

    One of those choices for us was getting an early discharge. Everyone was happy to help move us along so we were able to come home about 30 hours after delivery. It was an "ideal" uncomplicated delivery and my newborn boy is the picture of health, so it was just a matter of standard tests and paperwork to get through. Even though, or perhaps because, the center was full, we were ready to head home even earlier than originally expected.

    It would be a 5 star experience if it weren't for the food. Yeah, I know it's called room service just because it comes to your room, but don't ever think of those trays as bringing anything close to actual resort food. Again, with what your bill ends up being, you'd think a bit more could be done about the little things. The food honestly played a role in our decision to come home a day early. It was all bad. Except for the cinnamon roll, which was supposed to be a croissant, but the roll was ok. Like a decent packaged gas station pastry. The served coffee is also atrocious, but there's a lovely Finger Lakes counter in the lobby. Of course, that's not included nor does it come to your room. There really should be free decent (strong) coffee for all the new dads.

    Regardless of needing to arrange meal deliveries from the outside, if we have a third child, that child will have a "Strong Beginning".
