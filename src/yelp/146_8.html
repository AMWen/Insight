For 3 scheduled surgeries, I was kept overnight, once in the post-op recovery area (just a big open room with lots of activity day and night) and twice stowed in a cramped exam room that was too small for the bed. Two more times I waited 5 and 12 hours respectively to get into a room. Food is a major problem. On one occasion I wound up not eating for 36 hours--nothing after midnight before surgery, delayed surgery, and by the time I was in recovery, the kitchen was closed and the nurses had literally nothing available; the following AM, because I was marooned in recovery, not in a room, it took hours and repeated pleading to get something to eat. On other occasions, it's simply that there is no good method of communication with the kitchen, no reliable means of deliveries, and limited hours. I urge anyone headed here to bring emergency rations (energy bars, e.g.) with them. Some of the rooms are OK. A surprising number of them are very poorly designed, exemplified by lighting which the patients cannot turn on or off themselves. The patients hate this; the floor staff hate it. And still it persists. Last time through, I explained to my doctor that if I needed further elective surgery, I would be sorry to have to change physicians, but that I would not return voluntarily to Methodist. But at the moment, although Methodist is only blocks from my apartment, I would almost certainly go to NYU, Columbia, or Sinai for anything that could possibly result in an overnight stay.

I urge anyone headed here to bring emergency rations (energy bars, e.g.) with them.

Specialties

NewYork-Presbyterian Brooklyn Methodist Hospital is a not-for-profit, 651-bed acute-care teaching hospital in the Park Slope section of Brooklyn. NYPBMH has Institutes in the following areas: Advanced and Minimally Invasive Surgery, Asthma and Lung Disease; Cancer Care; Cardiology and Cardiac Surgery; Digestive and Liver Disorders; Diabetes and Other Endocrine Disorders; Vascular Medicine and Surgery; Family Care; Neurosciences; Orthopedic Medicine and Surgery and Women's Health. NewYork-Presbyterian Brooklyn Methodist Hospital is affiliated with the Weill Cornell Medical College and is a member of the NewYork-Presbyterian Healthcare System.

History

Established in 1881.

The story of New York Methodist Hospital goes back to the 1880s, when the Reverend James Monroe Buckley, having lost a dear friend in a tragic accident, persuaded the Methodist Church to build a hospital in Brooklyn. The Hospital, founded in 1881, has undergone extensive renovation and modernization over the years.
