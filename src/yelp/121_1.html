Warning: there's a very very very good chance you'll have a roommate while in recovery.  I sincerely hope yours is better than mine was.  The size of room is OK but chances are you'll have the curtains drawn so it'll feel smaller. The bathroom was pretty small and the the bed was standard issue. Nurses in recovery were mixed. I had some truly fantastic nurses who checked in on me even before I had to use the call button, and then I had a couple that made me want to cry.  

    With any luck you'll get to head home after a couple days and the checkout process is easy.  The lead nurse comes by to go over paperwork, makes sure you have a pediatrician lined up, and make sure you are feeling OK about going home w/ a new little human.  Mine also brought a big bag of goods to go home with: diapers, formula, and a nose aspirator.  I'm sure you've read on mommy sites/blogs where they tell you to take everything the hospital gives you - YOU SHOULD TAKE EVERYTHING THE HOSPITAL GIVES YOU!  I took the extra blankets in his bassinet, all the diapers, any 'mommy recovery' items I could get my hands on, etc etc etc. Take. It. All.

    A couple other misc notes:

    The food is bad. Over the span of 3 days I ate/drank challah rolls, apple sauce, tea and cranberry juice.  Basically nothing else was edible.  There are a bunch of places around the hospital for real food - a couple of our nurses had deli and pizza shop suggestions for us.  Oh!  and if like me you find yourself super dehydrated - recovery has 2 water/ice chip machines AND a small room w/ a fridge stocked with apple/cranberry juices and little milk cartons.  I raided that twice during my stay (why are hospitals SO dry!?)

    Parking in the neighborhood is TERRIBLE.  So use the parking garage.  Pretty sure there's a flat rate and it wasn't too bad.  But if you can - avoid coming/going at 7am and 7pm b/c that's when the hospital staff has their shift change and getting in/out of the lot will take for-e-ver.

    Maimonides Birthing Center is a female only wing after 9pm - so any male significant others must leave.  I didn't love this but it does allow the hubby's to go home and finish getting the apartment ready to bring a baby home.
