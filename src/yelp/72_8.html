My after care in the hospital was awful, people running in and out of my room trying to sell stuff or get me to sign over my placenta, or put me on WIC. I was not helped to the restroom, or out of the bed, or to walk around, I had to do everything on my own until my husband came in. There is nowhere for your family to stay either so every night my husband had to go home. How is that for family bonding? The nicest and most understanding people were the housecleaners and the food servers. They circumcised my son and didn't tell me  how to care for it. When we went to the pediatrician days later he said the skin was about to reattach itself because we didn't know how to care for it. I asked for information on how to care for my c section on day 2 and was told they don't give anything until checkout, well on checkout I never got that information. Also upon leaving I had to wait 30 mins for a wheelchair and when he came he dropped me off at the security guard station inside the hospital and not at the car, I had to get up and walk to the car with my son in my arms. They also didn't check the car seat either. All in all there are more things to complain about and I am not from NY and only went here because my midwife used to work there, but If you are wanting a nice birth DO NOT GO TO THIS HOSPITAL!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!.

History

Established in 1845.

The Brooklyn Hospital Center is in the business of keeping people healthy. When you arrive in need of care, we move quickly to help you start healing so that you can return home. As Brooklyn's first hospital, we offer a full spectrum of health care services with an outstanding team of dedicated doctors, nurses and staff, all working hard to get you back to your family as soon as possible.

And if experience matters--we've been doing this longer than any other hospital in Brooklyn!

We are affiliated with the NewYork-Presbyterian Healthcare System through the hospital's affiliation with Cornell University's Weill Cornell Medical College.
