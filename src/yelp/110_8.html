As you may have gathered from my review below, I developed a kidney infection while I was visiting a friend in New York. Thinking it was appendicitis and overwhelmed with discomfort and weakness, I ended up going to the nearest emergency room. I was seen by the triage nurse almost immediately, and was then given a bed right away. All the nurses and doctors were relatively young, nice, and very helpful. The PA that treated me was actually wearing jeans, a T-shirt, Converse, and had visible tattoos on her arms. Some people may have found her attire unprofessional (everyone else was wearing scrubs), but I thought it was actually refreshing. And the male nurse that was helping me was incredibly nice (and kinda cute--too bad I looked like I had been run over by a Mac truck). The only negative thing I have to say about this place is that at one point the PA came in to tell me that my blood test showed that my white blood cell count was high but not high enough to indicate a kidney infection, so they decided is was just a bad UTI. Then they pumped me full of two bags of saline and a bag of antiobiotics (via IV), at which point I began to feel very ill again. The PA returned a while later and told me she accidentally looked at the wrong chart before and that my white blood cell count was in fact 17 (it's normally 9 or 10), meaning that I had a pretty bad infection and that my immune system was overhauling. I found this to be a little worrisome--it's never good to look at the wrong chart (and much worse to admit it to the patient!). But this didn't deter me from giving them a 5-star rating--I can overlook the mistake. They actually wanted to discharge me a few hours before I ended up leaving, but I still wasn't feeling well and asked if I could just stay there and rest. They gave me a blanket and a makeshift pillow (and even an egg-salad sandwich) and let me be for a couple hours. When I finally felt well enough to leave they gave me printouts with everything I needed to know about my kidney infection and the medication I was prescribed. I hope to never have to return to an ER in New York (or ever again, for that matter), but if I do I'll only go here.

Specialties

Combining medical excellence with clinical innovation, we are a 799-bed teaching hospital, founded in 1889, located on Manhattan's Lower East Side. History

Established in 1889.
