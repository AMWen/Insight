I had two pregnancy related stays at SNCH this year. The first was in July due to a bleed I was experiencing. The staff was fantastic. Taking the time to explain the different procedures and why they were being done. I met a number of doctors including high-risk and neonatal. Very patient and thorough. Nursing staff was great too. I have a fear of needles and they were in incredibly gentle. Fast forward to me delivery in August. I was scheduled for a c-section but started to dilate the day before. As such it was moved up a day. Luckily the staff remembered my huge fear of needles and were gentle getting me set up. One of the doctor's was quite reassuring throughout the process and really helped to keep me calm despite the accelerated plan. The anesthesiologist was AMAZING! Barely felt the needle going in. The Lactation Consultants and nurses kept encouraging me to pump to get my supply up. The cheerleading was needed as my baby was in NICU so I could not breastfeed directly at first and the baby had to be fed breastmilk in bottles. Special thank you to Dr. Jean-Charles for his thoroughness with my daughter's health in NICU. One thing to note is that if the baby is in NICU you do not have access to them 24/7. There are 2 one-hour windows where the staff is changing from one shift to the next. The staff would ask me to leave during that time. Understandable so they could change, transfer notes, etc. however I always missed that feeding time window with my child. And the nurses would say she got super fussy so they fed her during that time. As far as I'm concerned every minute and opportunity to interact with your child when they are in NICU counts. It's hard enough with them being in NICU in an incubator thereby having limited interactions with them. Being asked to leave your child for a shift change though just stings. Again, not the end of the world for me but it still hurt. Overall, I would highly recommend this hospital for maternity.

Specialties

For every accident and illness, for every health challenge you face, and for support in helping you stay as healthy as possible, South Nassau is dedicated to serving you. We're your neighbors here on the South Shore.

We have an impressive range of services both at our hospital and at our many locations in the communities we serve. But our clearest commitment comes in the form of people -- the staff and physicians who have made your health care their life's work.

And so our mission is clear: to bring you a full range of high-quality health care right here, close to your work, home, family and friends. Connect with us today.

History

Established in 1928.

At South Nassau Communities Hospital, we are at the center of a health care renaissance: one that's squarely focused on the health and well-being of you and your family. Providing superior, quality patient care is at the center of all we do here at South Nassau. We are dedicated to making your time with us as comfortable as possible, providing you with the state-of-the-art health services you need, together with the compassionate care you deserve. We believe that our employees and physicians are our most valued resources. Call us today for more information.
