Had a knee replaced here. Was very impressed with everything and everyone, I came into contact with here. This hospital is outstanding!!!!

I'm in my 50s and had horrible pain and a hip replacement was the only solution. I wanted the Anterior approach but the only doc at the Baptist that I found was unable to do it for months. So I went to another and explained that my primary goal was to be active as soon as possible. I was told that he doesn't do the Anterior approach but I could have minimally invasive and "you'll be walking and driving in 4 weeks and will be able to do everything you ever did. I had told him that I did Pilates, treadmill, bike, yoga and weight lifting and worked full time. After the surgery I discovered that I have ALL the limitations of the traditional hip replacement procedure- I can't put on my own socks and shoes, can't bend forward,can't do anything but the minor things the PT lets me do. I can't sit in almost every chair in my home, need an elevated toilet seat and special tools to get dressed. I have no idea how long these restrictions will be in place but the PT says that many of my previous exercises will be precluded forever and other will require modifications. I can't sleep at night due to the positions you're required to be in. I have no idea when I can return to work or have a life back and there's no information until the 6 week appointment and no follow up to even see how you're doing. Very discouraging and disappointing. Now I sit at home and wait for the magic 6 weeks to see when I can have a life again. If you're considering a hip replacement, go somewhere that they'll do the Anterior procedure and don't settle for the Posterior approach...you'll regret it very much.
