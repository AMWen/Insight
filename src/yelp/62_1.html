Specialties

The NuHealthSystem is a $575M integrated health care delivery system that encompasses the Nassau University Medical Center, a 530 bed Level I trauma center and teaching hospital, the A. Holly Patterson Extended Care Facility, a 589 bed skilled nursing home and five family health centers, co-operated with the Li federally Qualified Health Centers.  It is NAssau's only public hospital and safety net health system, providing a wide range of quality and affordable health care to Nassau's most vulnerable populations.  NuHealth offers medical, mental health and substance abuse services, as well as specialized care for a wide range of health conditions in the hospital's numerous outpatient specialty clinics, in addition to the various clinics for primary and preventive care.

History

Established in 1935.

NUMC started as Meadowbrook Hospital in 1935 and expanded in 1974 as Nassau University Medical Center, treating everyone regardless of ability to pay.
