The emergency room is still brutal in the sense that if your situation is so not dire the conditions can be uncomfortable. I would only go if your need is dire, if you cannot go to urgent care, and if you cannot be adequately cared for by a community hospital. I would not go there for routine care as a stated before. As noted in the Boston Globe your average outcome for a typical run of the mill procedure like a hernia or a childbirth will be the same as in a typical community hospital. However for rare and complex conditions they truly excel. They increasingly practice "evidence based medicine" which for us lay people means better outcomes. It still is very expensive - no doubt more so. And you would be wise to check that your insurance covers it.     MGH is an exceptionally mixed bag. On the one hand you will find a level of expertise in manyâ€¦ MGH is an exceptionally mixed bag. On the one hand you will find a level of expertise in many disciplines that is virtually unmatched. If you have a rare disease, require difficult to find treatment, or need to see someone in a rare subspecialty, you have come to the right place. If no one else can treat your ailment or save your life and it's medically feasible, MGH is the place for you.

    Unfortunately along with its high prestige, it's Harvard medical students and residents, it's pick of the litter nurses, and the latest in medical technology, is an arrogance and rudeness that you have to experience to appreciate. The receptionists in many departments (and I have had experience with nine of them) are incredibly rude, even by Boston standards. Sometimes they will not return phonecalls. At other times they will cancel appointments without notice. They may deny receiving faxes from other doctors that they have most certainly received.

    Not all of the physicians are that great. Some are mediocre. Many will not return phonecalls even regarding urgent matters. Some of the doctors have brought condescension to an art form and delight at intimidating the luckless patient - especially inpatient. The hospital is very picky about taking patients as transfers from community hospitals. Even if you badly need a higher level of care they may choose not to take you as a transfer. Parking is very expensive and the traffic is legendary - Cambridge Street is ground zero for selfish and aggressive Boston drivers. If you are well enough to travel using the T, I would advise you to do so.

    The Boston Globe mentioned that for many run of the mill procedures, MGH is very expensive despite not having better patient outcomes than community hospitals. A 2008 Boston Globe investigation found that providers such as Partners (of which MGH is a part) were paid 15 to 60 percent more than most of their competitors for surgeries, MRIs, X-rays, and other medical services. If you merely require routine maternity care, you will not get better care at MGH than at a reputable community hospital. This was evidenced by Boston Globe reporting that showed that mothers and new born babies did not experience better outcomes at MGH versus community hospitals. Consequently, I would strongly advise you not to visit for routine care.

    Beware of the extreme expense of this facility. I was quoted $10,000 a night for a regular hospital bed before insurance discount. This means if someone did not have insurance they would be expected to pay $100,000 for a 10 day stint + doctors fees + meds + miscellaneous extras (I am sure the price has since risen). Yes of course the negotiated insurance rate is more than two thirds lower. Nevertheless they will insist that someone without insurance or out of network pay this exorbitant rate despite the fact that they are least able to afford it. You face financial peril if your insurance refuses to pay. I have asked the hospital on multiple occasions if I can merely pay the negotiated insurance rate in the event that my insurance declines to pay and each time they flatly refused. This means that you may have to fork over somewhere between 50% and 300% more than the amount your insurance company would have paid. I would strongly advise you to consider this financial consideration if you have any reason to believe that you insurance company may deny coverage.

    All hail the ethics of MGH who worship at capitalism's feet and run roughshod over the economically unfortunate. (Note: the Medicare rate is substantially below even the negotiated insurance rate so they are still making oodles of profit at $3,000 a night.) You might find it unsurprising that they have a VIP service for wealthy donors who give millions of dollars a year to the the hospital.

    The finance department can be famously difficult to deal with. Even if they misbilled the insurance company, they invariably don't want to accept responsibility for sorting the problem out. It is incredible that this Harvard affiliated hospital is one of the richest in the USA (possibly the richest) and yet is ruthless in ringing out money from patients that clearly cannot afford it. At this point I would draw your attention to the billions of dollars they were able to fundraise from exceptionally wealthy donors resulting in the newly constructed towers.

    If you have good insurance, can afford the copays and deductibles, have all your preaithorizations in order, and are very ill with a disease that cannot be treated elsewhere, you probably should come to MGH. Otherwise you may prefer a more personable hospital where the doctors treat you with much greater dignity and respect, and where you are less likely to face sky high hospital bills.     Read more
